Life Expectancy

by Ong Jeng Yue

Life expectancy refers to the number of years a person is expected to live based on the statistical average. Life expectancy captures the mortality along the entire life course. Since the 19th century, there has been a huge improvement in life expectancy of humans all around the world. However, the inequality of life expectancy is still very large across and within countries. In 2019 itself, the country with the lowest life expectancy is Central African Republic with 53 years, whereas the life expectancy of Japan is 30 years longer.

Change in Life Expectancy over time.

The two maps below show how Life Expectancy has improved over the last 29 years.

Improvements can be seen all over the over. However, the most noticeable difference is in the poverty struck African continent. Despite fighting against extreme poverty, the entire African continent managed to improve their life expectancy from and average of low 50's to 60 and even achieving 70 years in some countries.

Projection of Life Expectancy.

Over the last 29 years, life expectancy of every country seems to have grown by a reputable amount. The question is how long will this growth continue?
The visualisation below presents the United Nation's prediction of life expectancy until the year 2099.

Due to the improvements in the medical field, the life expectancy of human is expected to keep growing as time goes on. However, will there be a limit as to how long humans can live?
